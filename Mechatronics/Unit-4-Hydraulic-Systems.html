1. Introduction to Hydraulic Systems & Pascal's Law

PYQ Focus

Explain Pascal's law. Explain the components of a hydraulic system with a neat diagram. Explain various components of an hydraulic system.

A. What is a Hydraulic System?

A Hydraulic System uses pressurized, incompressible liquid (usually special hydraulic oil) to transmit force, power, and motion.

Easy Word Explanation

Think of hydraulics as "Liquid Muscle." Because you cannot squish or compress water/oil, if you push oil in at one end of a pipe, it will immediately push out with massive force at the other end.

Real-Life Examples: The heavy lifting arms of a JCB excavator, car brakes, and airplane landing gear.

B. Pascal's Law PYQ

The entire foundation of hydraulic systems relies on Pascal's Law.

Statement:

"Pressure applied to any part of a confined, incompressible fluid is transmitted equally and undiminished in all directions, acting with equal force on equal areas."

Formula: P = F / A (Pressure = Force / Area)
How it helps:

By using a small cylinder to push fluid into a very large cylinder, you can multiply your force massively. Pushing with 10 kg of force on a small piston can lift a 1000 kg car on a large piston!

C. Components of a Hydraulic System PYQ

Diagram Description (To draw in Exam):

Draw a block diagram in this sequence:

[ Electric Motor ] [ Hydraulic Pump ] [ Control Valves ] [ Actuator (Cylinder) ] [ Load ]

(Below the pump, draw a tank [ Reservoir ] and connect a pipe from the tank to the pump, and a return pipe from the valves back to the tank).

Electric Motor / Engine

Provides the initial mechanical power to spin the pump.

Hydraulic Pump (The Heart)

Sucks oil from the tank and pushes it into the pipes at high pressure.

Control Valves (Traffic Cops)

Controls the pressure, flow rate, and direction of the pressurized oil.

Actuators (The Muscles)

Converts the fluid energy back into mechanical movement (e.g., a hydraulic cylinder pushing a heavy block).

Reservoir (Tank)

Stores the hydraulic oil, cools it down, and allows air bubbles to escape.

Supporting Elements

Filters (to remove dirt from oil), Hoses/Pipes (to carry oil).

2. Hydraulic Pumps & Power Packs

PYQ Focus

Main function and role of a hydraulic pump. Compare gear, vane, and piston pump. Construction and working of balanced vane pump.

A. Role and Main Function of a Hydraulic Pump PYQ

  • Function: A hydraulic pump converts mechanical energy (from a rotating electric motor) into hydraulic energy (fluid flow and pressure).
  • Role in the System: It acts as the "Heart" of the system. It creates a vacuum at its inlet to suck oil from the reservoir and physically pushes the oil out through its outlet to overcome the resistance of the load.

Note: Pumps create FLOW, not pressure. Pressure is created by the resistance to that flow.

PYQ Focus

B. Construction and Working of a Balanced Vane Pump

A vane pump uses sliding blades (vanes) inside a rotor to move fluid. A "Balanced" vane pump is an improved design.

Construction (For Exam Diagram):

Draw an elliptical (oval-shaped) outer cam ring. Inside it, draw a perfectly circular rotor with slots. Inside the slots, draw rectangular vanes pushed outward by springs. Draw TWO inlets directly opposite each other, and TWO outlets directly opposite each other.

Working Principle:

  1. The electric motor spins the central rotor. Centrifugal force pushes the vanes outwards to constantly touch the inner surface of the oval cam ring.
  2. Because the ring is oval, the space between the rotor and the ring increases and decreases twice per rotation.
  3. Where the space increases, a vacuum is formed, sucking oil IN (Inlet).
  4. Where the space decreases, the oil is squeezed and pushed OUT (Outlet).
Why "Balanced"?

Because it has two inlets and two outlets perfectly opposite each other, the high-pressure forces pushing against the rotor cancel each other out. This means zero side-load on the bearings, extending the pump's life massively!

C. Comparison: Gear, Vane, and Piston Pumps PYQ Table

D. Hydraulic Power Pack

A Hydraulic Power Pack is a standalone, compact unit that contains the Reservoir, Electric Motor, Pump, and basic relief valves all mounted together in one box. You just plug it into the wall, connect two hoses to your machine, and you have instant hydraulic power.

3. Valves: Direction, Pressure, and Flow Control

PYQ Focus

Classification of valves. Direction control valves working & types. Spool vs Poppet valve. 4/2 & 4/3 DCV symbols. Commonly used pressure control valves.

Valves are the "Traffic Cops" of the hydraulic system. They are classified into three main types:

Direction Control (DCV)

Directs where the oil goes.

Pressure Control

Controls how hard the oil pushes.

Flow Control

Controls how fast the oil moves.

A. Direction Control Valves (DCV) & Types PYQ

Working Principle: A DCV works by opening, closing, or changing the internal paths connecting various ports. It uses a moving internal mechanism to block fluid from entering one pipe and route it into another, allowing an actuator to extend, retract, or stop.

Types of DCV based on Internal Mechanism:

Poppet Valve

Uses a small ball or a cone pressed against a circular seat by a spring to block flow. When a lever is pushed, the ball is forced off the seat, letting fluid pass.

Advantage: Zero leakage because the pressure forces the cone tighter into the seat.

Spool Valve

Uses a solid cylindrical metal shaft (spool) with thick sections (lands) and thin sections (grooves) sliding inside a tube. As it slides, grooves connect different ports, lands block others.

Advantage: A single spool can easily control complex 4-way, 5-way routing without massive force.

B. Symbolic Representation of DCVs (4/2 and 4/3 DCV) PYQ

In hydraulic circuit diagrams, DCVs are drawn using squares (boxes). The number of boxes equals the number of "Positions". The number of connection points on one box equals the number of "Ports".
Ports are usually: P (Pump), T (Tank/Reservoir), A (Actuator side A), B (Actuator side B).

1. 4/2 DCV (4 Ports, 2 Positions)

Explanation: It has 2 square boxes side-by-side.

  • Inside Box 1, draw an arrow from P → A, and an arrow from B → T.
  • Inside Box 2, draw an arrow from P → B, and an arrow from A → T.

Usage: Used to push a cylinder fully outward, and then fully backward. There is no stopping in the middle.

2. 4/3 DCV (4 Ports, 3 Positions)

Explanation: It has 3 square boxes side-by-side.

  • Left Box: Arrow P → A, B → T (Extend).
  • Right Box: Arrow P → B, A → T (Retract).
  • Center Box: All ports blocked with a 'T' symbol (⊥), meaning fluid cannot go anywhere.

Usage: Used to extend, retract, AND stop the cylinder in the middle of its stroke.

C. Pressure Control Valves PYQ

If fluid pressure gets too high, pipes will explode. Pressure control valves protect the system.

Pressure Relief Valve (Most Common)

It is installed immediately after the pump. It acts as a safety fuse. If a machine gets jammed, pressure builds up. Once the pressure hits the safe limit (e.g., 200 bar), the relief valve forces a spring open and dumps the excess oil safely back into the tank.

Pressure Reducing Valve

Used when one specific part of the machine requires a lower pressure than the main system. It reduces the pressure locally.

4. Actuators and Supporting Elements

PYQ Focus

Define actuation and how actuation systems are used.

A. Define Actuation and Actuation Systems PYQ

Definition: Actuation is the physical process of triggering a mechanical movement or causing a machine part to operate. An Actuation System is the combination of devices (like pumps, valves, and actuators) that work together to convert stored energy (electrical, hydraulic, or pneumatic) into direct mechanical work.

How they are used (Examples):

1. Heavy Machinery

A hydraulic actuation system in a dump truck pushes a massive cylinder to tilt the bed and dump sand.

2. Mechatronics/Robotics

An electrical actuation system (servo motor) is used in an industrial robotic arm to rotate a joint by exactly 45 degrees.

3. Manufacturing

Used in CNC machines to hydraulically clamp a block of metal firmly onto the cutting table so it doesn't move during milling.

B. Types of Hydraulic Actuators

Linear Actuators (Hydraulic Cylinders)

Provide push-and-pull straight-line motion.

Single-Acting Cylinder

Oil pushes the piston outward. A mechanical spring pushes it back in when oil pressure is released.

Double-Acting Cylinder

Oil is pushed into Port A to extend the piston, and then oil is pushed into Port B to forcefully retract the piston.

Rotary Actuators (Hydraulic Motors)

Provide continuous rotational motion (spinning). They work exactly like a pump but in reverse (oil goes in, causes the shaft to spin, oil goes out).

Basic Circuit Design: Controlling a Double-Acting Cylinder

Safety First:

4
The Brain:

Connect the main pressure line to the 'P' port of a 4/3 Direction Control Valve (DCV). Connect the 'T' port of the DCV back to the Tank.

5
The Muscle:

Connect port 'A' of the DCV to the rear of a Double-Acting Cylinder. Connect port 'B' of the DCV to the front of the cylinder.

Circuit Logic:
  • When the operator shifts the valve to Position 1, oil flows P → A, pushing the cylinder out. Oil in the front escapes B → T.
  • When shifted to Position 2, oil flows P → B, pushing the cylinder back in.
  • When in the Center Position, all ports are blocked, locking the cylinder firmly in place!
