1. Stepper Motors

PYQ Focus

Explain with neat sketch working of permanent magnet stepper motor. Name two applications of stepper motors in mechatronic systems.

A. What is a Stepper Motor?

A Stepper Motor is a brushless DC electric motor that divides a full rotation into a number of equal, discrete "steps." Unlike a regular DC motor that spins continuously when power is applied, a stepper motor rotates by a specific angle (e.g., 1.8°) for every electrical pulse it receives.

Easy Word Explanation

Think of a regular motor as a smooth-flowing river, and a stepper motor as the ticking hands of a wall clock. It moves precisely tick-by-tick (step-by-step).

B. Working Principle of Permanent Magnet (PM) Stepper Motor PYQ

1. Diagram Description (For Exam):
  • Draw a central circular rotor labeled with N and S poles (indicating a permanent magnet).
  • Draw four electromagnetic stators (coils wrapped around iron cores) surrounding the rotor at 90-degree intervals (Top, Bottom, Left, Right). Label them A, B, C, D.
2. Working Principle:
  • The rotor is a permanent magnet. The stator consists of electromagnets.
  • The motor works on the basic magnetic principle: Unlike poles attract, and Like poles repel.
  • When phase A (Top stator) is energized, it becomes a North pole. It attracts the South pole of the rotor, locking it into position.
  • To make the motor step, the controller turns OFF phase A and turns ON phase B (Right stator). The rotor is magnetically pulled and turns 90 degrees clockwise to align with phase B.
  • By turning the stator electromagnets ON and OFF in a specific sequence (A → B → C → D), the magnetic field rotates, dragging the permanent magnet rotor along with it in precise steps.

C. Applications of Stepper Motors PYQ

Since stepper motors provide highly precise positioning without needing a feedback sensor, they are widely used in:

3D Printers & CNC

To move the printing nozzle accurately by fractions of a millimeter.

Hard Disk Drives

To precisely position the magnetic read/write head over the spinning disk.

Robotics

Used in the joints of small robotic arms for precise angular movement.

2. Servo Drives and Servo Motors

PYQ Focus

Define a servo drive. Describe the working principle of a servo drive system/DC servo motor. Write advantages and disadvantages of Servomotor. Discuss the mechanical aspects of motor selection.

A. What is a Servo Drive / Servo Motor? PYQ

  • Servo Motor: A rotary or linear actuator that allows for precise control of angular or linear position, velocity, and acceleration.
  • Servo Drive: A special electronic amplifier used to power electric servomechanisms. It monitors the feedback signal from the motor and continuously adjusts the voltage and current to correct any errors.

Easy Word Explanation

A stepper motor runs blind (Open-loop). A Servo Motor runs with its "eyes open" (Closed-loop). If you tell a servo motor to turn 90 degrees, it turns, looks at an internal sensor to confirm it hit exactly 90 degrees, and corrects itself if it missed.

PYQ Focus

B. Working Principle of D.C. Servo Motor System

A DC Servo motor is essentially a normal DC motor coupled with a sensor (encoder) and a sophisticated PID controller circuit.

Block Diagram (To draw in Exam):

[ Input Command ] → [ Error Detector ] → [ Servo Amplifier ] → [ DC Motor ] → [ Load ]
(Draw a feedback line from the Motor/Load back to the Error Detector, passing through an [ Encoder/Sensor ])

Working Principle:

  1. The user inputs a desired position (Setpoint).
  2. The Encoder (sensor attached to the motor shaft) constantly reads the actual position of the motor and sends this feedback to the Error Detector.
  3. The Error Detector compares the actual position with the desired position and calculates the Error Signal.
  4. The Servo Amplifier boosts this weak error signal and sends power to the DC Motor.
  5. The motor turns in the direction that reduces the error. Once the error reaches zero (motor is at the exact target), the amplifier stops the power, and the motor brakes instantly.

C. Advantages

  • High Precision: Feedback guarantees 100% accurate positioning.
  • High Speed and Torque: Can operate at very high speeds without losing steps (unlike stepper motors).
  • High Efficiency: Consumes power only proportional to the load.

Disadvantages

  • High Cost: Much more expensive than stepper motors due to the complex encoder and drive electronics.
  • Tuning Required: The PID controller must be carefully tuned; otherwise, the motor will vibrate/oscillate.

D. Mechanical Aspects of Motor Selection PYQ

When an engineer buys a motor for a mechatronics project, they must analyze:

Torque Requirements

How much heavy lifting/turning force does the machine need? (Starting vs. Running torque).

Speed Requirements

What is the maximum RPM (Revolutions Per Minute) needed?

Inertia Matching

The inertia of the motor rotor must be matched closely to the load for smooth starts/stops.

Physical Size & Weight

Does the motor fit inside the machine chassis?

Operating Environment

Does it need to be waterproof, dustproof, or heat resistant?

3. Cams and Followers

PYQ Focus

Draw the schematic diagram of a CAM and explain its functioning. What is a follower? Explain different types of cam. Explain how CAMS are classified.

A. What are Cams and Followers? PYQ

  • Cam: A mechanical component, usually an irregularly shaped rotating wheel or cylinder, used to transmit variable motion to another part.
  • Follower: A mechanical part that is held against the surface of the cam. As the cam rotates, the irregular shape of the cam forces the follower to move up and down (oscillate or translate).

Easy Word Explanation

Think of a Cam as a wheel with a "bump" on it. When the wheel spins, the bump pushes up a rod (the follower) resting on it.

Real-Life Example: Inside a car engine, the camshaft spins and the "bumps" (cams) push the engine valves (followers) open to let fuel in and exhaust out at exact timings.
PYQ Focus

B. Functioning and Schematic Diagram

Diagram Description:
  • Draw a circular wheel with an egg-like extension on one side (This is the Radial Cam). Draw a center axis dot.
  • Resting directly on top of this wheel, draw a vertical rod with a pointed or roller end (This is the Follower).
  • Draw a spring pushing the follower down to ensure it always touches the cam.

Functioning:

The cam is connected to a motor and rotates at a constant speed. Due to its irregular egg-like profile, the distance from the center axis to the edge constantly changes. As the wider part of the cam rotates under the follower, it pushes the follower upwards. As the narrow part comes around, the spring pushes the follower back down. Result: Rotary motion is converted into precise linear up-down motion.

C. Classification / Types of Cams PYQ

1. Radial / Plate Cam

A flat, plate-like cam. The follower moves in a direction perpendicular to the axis of rotation of the cam. (Most common).

2. Cylindrical Cam

A cylinder with a groove cut into its curved surface. The follower rides inside this groove, moving parallel to the axis of rotation.

3. Wedge Cam

A wedge shape that moves back and forth linearly, pushing a follower up and down.

4. Camshaft-Based vs. Electronic Cams

PYQ Focus

Compare camshaft-based systems and electronic cam systems.

In modern mechatronics, heavy mechanical cams are being replaced by "Electronic Cams."

  • Mechanical Camshaft: A physical solid metal shaft with shaped metal lumps.
  • Electronic Cam (E-Cam): A system where a Servo Motor replaces the physical metal cam. A computer program tells the servo motor exactly how fast and far to move to perfectly mimic the motion of a physical cam.
Feature Mechanical Camshaft Electronic Cam (E-Cam)
Physical Form Solid, heavy, precisely machined metal. A servo motor controlled by a software program.
Flexibility / Changes Zero flexibility. To change the motion, you must throw away the metal cam and manufacture a new one. Highly flexible. To change the motion, the engineer just types a new code into the computer.
Wear and Tear High friction leads to physical wear and tear over time. Virtually no mechanical wear since the motion profile is software-based.
Speed and Vibration At very high speeds, mechanical followers can "bounce" off the cam, causing severe vibration. Smooth, mathematical motion profiles eliminate bouncing and vibration.

5. Tool Magazines and Indexing Mechanisms

PYQ Focus

State the function of a tool magazine in CNC. Explain tool magazine types. Explain construction of disc type tool magazine. Define indexing mechanism. Explain ratchet and pawl mechanism.

A. Tool Magazine in CNC Machines PYQ

Function: A Tool Magazine is a storage unit integrated into a CNC (Computer Numerical Control) machine. Its primary function is to securely hold multiple different cutting tools (like drills, milling cutters, taps) and deliver the correct tool to the Automatic Tool Changer (ATC) arm exactly when the machining program requests it.

Easy Word Explanation

It is like an artist's pencil box. Instead of the human operator stopping the machine to change the drill bit manually, the machine automatically picks a new bit from the "magazine" and keeps working.

B. Types of Tool Magazines

  • Disc/Drum Type: Tools are arranged in a circle on a rotating disc.
  • Chain Type: Used when a massive number of tools (50 to 100+) are needed. Tools are mounted on a long looping chain.

C. Construction of Disc Type PYQ

Diagram Description: Draw a large horizontal or vertical circular plate. Around the outer edge (periphery) of this plate, draw evenly spaced U-shaped slots or gripper clips. Inside these clips, draw various tools (drill bits). Draw a central motor that rotates this disc.

Construction & Working: It consists of a circular drum or disc driven by an indexing motor. The periphery holds spring-loaded tool grippers. When the CNC program calls for Tool #5, the central motor rotates the disc until Tool #5 aligns perfectly with the tool-changing arm. It is highly reliable but usually limited to holding 12 to 30 tools.

D. Indexing Mechanisms PYQ

Definition: An Indexing Mechanism is a mechanical device used to rotate a machine part (like a tool magazine or a workpiece table) by a highly precise, pre-defined mathematical angle, and then securely lock it in that position so an operation can be performed.

PYQ Focus

E. Ratchet and Pawl Mechanism

This is one of the simplest and most famous indexing/locking mechanisms.

Easy Word Explanation

It works exactly like a plastic zip-tie or the clicking sound you hear when a roller coaster climbs the first hill. It allows movement in only one direction and physically blocks movement in reverse.

Diagram Description:
  • Draw a gear wheel with asymmetrical teeth (teeth that are sloped on one side and flat/vertical on the other). This is the Ratchet.
  • Above the wheel, draw a small metal finger or lever pivoting on a pin, dropping into the gap between the teeth. This is the Pawl.
  • Draw a small spring pushing down on the Pawl.
Working Principle:

As the ratchet wheel rotates in the "forward" direction, the sloped side of the teeth pushes the pawl gently up and out of the way. The wheel spins freely. However, if the wheel tries to spin backward, the flat/vertical face of the tooth violently crashes into the pawl. The pawl firmly locks the wheel, preventing any reverse motion.
