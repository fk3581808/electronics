1. Introduction to Data Communication

PYQ Focus (Previous Year Question)

Draw the block diagram of data communication system and state the function of each block. Describe the computer network used in data communication.

A. What is Data Communication?

Data Communication is the exchange of data (in the form of 1s and 0s) between two devices via some form of transmission medium (like a wire cable or wireless signal). For communication to occur, the communicating devices must be part of a communication system made up of a combination of hardware and software.

Easy Word Explanation

Imagine you are talking to your friend on a phone call. Your voice is the 'data', the phone is the 'device', and the mobile network is the 'medium'. Data communication is just machines talking to each other instead of humans.

B. Block Diagram of a Data Communication System

A fundamental data communication system has 5 key components (Blocks):

[ SENDER ] ---> [ MESSAGE + PROTOCOL ] ---> [ MEDIUM ] ---> [ RECEIVER ]

Message

It is the information (data) to be communicated. Example: A text message, a JPEG photo, or an MP3 audio file.

Sender

The device that sends the data message. Example: Your smartphone, a computer, or an IoT temperature sensor.

Receiver

The device that receives the message. Example: A server, a smart TV, or your friend's laptop.

Transmission Medium

The physical path by which a message travels from sender to receiver. Example: Fiber optic cable, copper wire, or invisible Radio Waves (Wi-Fi).

Protocol

A set of rules that govern data communication. Without a protocol, two devices may be connected but cannot understand each other. Example: Just like two people must speak the same language (e.g., Hindi or English) to understand each other, computers use protocols like TCP/IP.

2. Concept of Analog and Digital Signals & Bandwidth

PYQ Focus

Differentiate between Analog and Digital signal using any four parameters. Explain with suitable waveforms.

Data must be converted into electromagnetic signals to travel across a network. These signals can be either Analog or Digital.

A. Analog Signals

An Analog Signal is a continuous wave that changes smoothly over time. It can have an infinite number of values within a range.

  • Waveform: It is typically represented by a Sine Wave (a smooth curve that goes up and down continuously).
Easy Ex: Think of an analog clock with moving hands or a fan regulator (dimmer) where you can smoothly increase speed from 0 to 5.
Real-Life: Human voice, temperature changes, old cassette tapes.

B. Digital Signals

A Digital Signal is a discrete signal that carries information in binary form (0s and 1s). It jumps suddenly between two voltage levels (e.g., +5V and 0V).

  • Waveform: It is represented by a Square Wave (sharp, flat-topped steps).
Easy Ex: Think of a simple light switch. It is either completely ON (1) or completely OFF (0). There is no "in-between."
Real-Life: Data stored in a hard drive, HDMI video signals, modern CPUs.

C. Differences Between Analog and Digital Signals PYQ

Parameter Analog Signal Digital Signal
1. Nature Continuous over time. Discrete (jumps between 0 and 1) over time.
2. Waveform Represented by a continuous Sine Wave. Represented by a step-like Square Wave.
3. Noise Susceptibility Highly affected by noise. Signal quality degrades over distance. Less affected by noise. Easy to regenerate accurately.
4. Bandwidth Requires less bandwidth. Requires more bandwidth.
5. Example Human voice, FM Radio signals. Computers, CDs, Digital watches.

D. What is Bandwidth?

Bandwidth is the maximum amount of data that can be transmitted over a network connection in a given amount of time. It is measured in Bits Per Second (bps), Mbps, or Gbps.

Easy Word Explanation (The Water Pipe)

Think of bandwidth as a water pipe. A wider pipe (high bandwidth) allows more water (data) to flow through at once than a narrow pipe (low bandwidth). A 100 Mbps connection will download a movie much faster than a 10 Mbps connection.

3. Computer Networks and Their Types

PYQ Focus

Define the computer network and brief different types. Explain Personal Area Networks and state two advantages and disadvantages.

A. What is a Computer Network?

A Computer Network is an interconnection of two or more computers or electronic devices so they can share resources (like printers, files, or an internet connection) and communicate with each other.

B. Types of Computer Networks (Based on Geography)

1. PAN (Personal Area Network) PYQ Focus

Definition: A network organized around an individual person within a very short range (typically within 10 meters).

Example: Connecting your wireless Bluetooth headphones to your smartphone, or a wireless mouse to a laptop.

Advantages:
(1) Highly secure because of short range (hard to hack from afar).
(2) Very low cost and no extra wiring needed.
Disadvantages:
(1) Very limited range (works only in same room).
(2) Slow data transfer speeds compared to LAN.

2. LAN (Local Area Network)

Definition: A network designed to operate over a small physical area, like an office, school, or a single building.

Example: The computer lab in your diploma college where all computers are connected to one main server using Ethernet cables.

3. MAN (Metropolitan Area Network)

Definition: Covers a larger geographic area, such as a city or a large campus. It connects multiple LANs together.

Example: Your local Cable TV network spread across your city, or a city-wide free public Wi-Fi network.

4. WAN (Wide Area Network)

Definition: Covers a large geographical area, such as a state, a country, or the world. Uses telecom links like satellites or deep-sea cables.

Example: The ATM network of the State Bank of India (all ATMs connected), or 4G/5G mobile networks.

5. Internetwork (The Internet)

Definition: An "Internetwork" is formed when two or more distinct networks (like multiple LANs and WANs) are connected together using routers. The biggest example is the Internet itself.

4. Computer Network Topologies

PYQ Focus

Describe different types in brief. Explain Mesh and Star topology. Explain Tree topology for a 3-storey building.

Topology refers to the physical or logical layout/arrangement of a network. It is how computers (nodes) are wired together.

[Image of computer network topologies]

1. Point-to-Point Topology

Contains exactly two hosts directly connected to each other using a single cable.

Ex: Connecting two laptops directly with one LAN cable.

2. Bus Topology

All devices are connected to a single, central main cable (backbone or bus).

Pros: Very cheap and easy to install.

Cons: If main cable breaks, entire network crashes.

PYQ

3. Star Topology

All devices connected to a central hub or switch. No direct connection between computers; data goes through the switch.

Pros: If one wire breaks, only that PC is lost. Easy to fix.

Cons: If central switch fails, the whole network goes down.

Analogy: Like a bicycle wheel; center axle is the switch, spokes are cables.

4. Ring Topology

Each device is connected to exactly two other devices, forming a circle/ring. Data travels in one direction.

Cons: If one computer fails, the chain breaks and network stops.

PYQ

5. Mesh Topology

Every single device is connected to every other device in the network directly.

Features:
Highly reliable: If a cable breaks, many other paths exist. Highly secure.
Cons:
Extremely expensive and complex to wire due to massive number of cables.

6. Tree Topology PYQ Focus: 3-Storey Building

Combination of Star and Bus topologies. Has a root node (main server) and all other nodes are connected in a hierarchy.

Explanation for a 3-Storey Building:
  • Ground Floor
    (Basement/Server Room): The main Server and the Main Router (Root Node) are placed here.
  • 1st Floor
    A central switch is placed here, connected to the ground floor router. 20 computers on the 1st floor are connected to this switch in a Star arrangement.
  • 2nd Floor
    Another switch is placed here, connected to the ground floor router. Computers on this floor connect to this switch.
  • 3rd Floor
    A third switch is placed here, connected down to the root router.

Why it's good: The building's network looks like a tree. If the 2nd-floor switch fails, only the 2nd floor loses the network. The 1st and 3rd floors keep working!

7. Daisy Chain Topology

Devices connected sequentially in a line (A -> B -> C -> D). Like a ring but not closed into a circle.

8. Hybrid Topology

Combination of two or more different topologies (e.g., combining a Star topology in one dept and Ring in another).

5. Network Architecture Models: OSI and TCP/IP

PYQ Focus

Compare OSI and TCP/IP. List the 7 layers of OSI and brief functions. Sketch layered architecture.

To allow computers from different companies (like Apple, Windows, Linux) to communicate, standard models were created.

A. The OSI Reference Model (Open Systems Interconnection)

Developed by ISO, it is a theoretical framework consisting of 7 distinct layers.

Easy Word Explanation (The Postal System)

Sending data is like sending a physical letter. The letter goes from you -> an envelope -> a post box -> a postman -> a truck -> an airplane -> the receiver. Each step does a specific job. The OSI model is the same, but for digital data.

[Image of OSI model 7 layers]

The 7 Layers of OSI (Top to Bottom)

Mnemonic: Please Do Not Throw Sausage Pizza Away
7
Application Layer (Top)

Provides user interface. Layer the user interacts with (Chrome, WhatsApp). Protocols: HTTP, FTP, SMTP.

6
Presentation Layer

Formats, encrypts, and compresses data. Translates JPEG, ASCII so receiver can read it.

5
Session Layer

Establishes, maintains, and ends communication sessions between computers (like phone call).

4
Transport Layer

Ensures complete data delivery. Breaks data into "Segments", retransmits lost data. Protocols: TCP, UDP.

3
Network Layer

Routes data from source to destination across multiple networks using IP Addresses. Routers work here.

2
Data Link Layer

Error-free transfer over physical layer. Packages bits into "Frames", uses MAC addresses. Switches work here.

1
Physical Layer (Bottom)

Deals with physical connection (cables, hubs). Converts digital 1s & 0s into electrical/optical signals over the wire.

B. TCP/IP Reference Model

This is the practical model that the modern Internet actually uses. It squeezed the 7 OSI layers into just 4 layers:

  • Network Access Layer: Combines OSI's Physical and Data Link layers.
  • Internet Layer: Equivalent to OSI's Network layer (Handles IP addressing and routing).
  • Transport Layer: Equivalent to OSI's Transport layer (Handles TCP/UDP).
  • Application Layer: Combines OSI's Session, Presentation, and Application layers.

C. Comparison: OSI vs TCP/IP PYQ

Feature OSI Model TCP/IP Model
Full Form Open Systems Interconnection. Transmission Control Protocol / Internet Protocol.
Number of Layers Has 7 distinct layers. Has 4 layers.
Nature It is a theoretical, reference model. It is a practical, widely implemented model (the Internet uses this).
Development Developed by ISO (International Org for Standardization). Developed by DARPA (US Department of Defense).
Strictness Strict boundary between layers. Looser boundaries; Application layer does many jobs.

6. Transmission Media: Wired and Wireless

PYQ Focus

Explain various transmission media. Compare guided and unguided. Analyse special features of wired vs wireless connectivity.

Transmission media is the physical path carrying the data. It is divided into two categories:

A. Guided Media (Wired)

Here, the signal is "guided" through a physical wire or cable.

  • Twisted Pair Cable Two insulated copper wires twisted to cancel out electromagnetic interference. (Cat6 LAN cables).
  • Coaxial Cable Central copper core surrounded by insulator and braided metal shield. (Cable TV).
  • Optical Fiber Cable (OFC) Uses glass/plastic threads to transmit data using pulses of light instead of electricity. Extremely high bandwidth.

B. Unguided Media (Wireless)

Data is transmitted through the air (or vacuum) using electromagnetic waves without physical cables.

  • Radio Waves Omnidirectional (travels in all directions). Penetrates walls easily. (FM Radio, Wi-Fi).
  • Microwaves Unidirectional (straight line). Sender/receiver must face each other. (Satellite comms, 5G).
  • Infrared Short-range. Cannot penetrate walls. (TV remote controls).

C. Comparison: Guided vs Unguided Media PYQ

Feature Guided (Wired) Unguided (Wireless)
Physical MediumUses physical cables (Copper, Glass).Uses air/vacuum to transmit waves.
DirectionDirected and point-to-point.Usually omnidirectional (broadcasts everywhere).
CostMore expensive (cable costs, digging).Cheaper to deploy over large areas.
SpeedExtremely high speed (Fiber Optics).Lower speeds compared to Fiber optics.
SecurityHighly secure. Harder to tap into wire.Less secure. Can be intercepted in air.

D. Special Features: Wired vs Wireless

Wired Features

  • Immunity to interference: A fiber optic cable under the ocean is not affected by thunderstorms.
  • Reliability: Constant speed. Gamers and stock traders use wired LANs because it doesn't "drop out."

Wireless Features

  • Mobility: Allows a user to walk around the building with their smartphone seamlessly.
  • Scalability: To add a new employee, just give the Wi-Fi password. (Wired requires physically running a new cable).
