1. Market and Marketing

PYQ Focus (Previous Year Question)

Define 'market'. Discuss the objective of marketing. Define marketing. Discuss the importance of marketing.

A. What is a 'Market'?

Traditionally, a Market is a physical place where buyers and sellers meet to exchange goods and services. However, in modern business, a market refers to the entire group of actual and potential buyers of a product or service.

Easy Word Explanation

The market isn't just a building or a shop; it is all the people who have the money and the desire to buy your product. If you make gaming PCs, all PC gamers in India are your "market."

B. What is 'Marketing'?

Marketing is the process of identifying, anticipating, and satisfying customer needs profitably. It involves everything a business does to acquire customers and maintain a relationship with them. It includes research, design, advertising, selling, and customer service.

Important Note: Selling is just exchanging a product for money. Marketing starts way before the product is even made (finding out what people want) and continues after the sale (customer feedback).

The Golden Rule of Marketing

Marketing is the art of making people fall in love with your product so much that selling becomes unnecessary.

C. Objectives of Marketing

Why do companies spend millions on marketing?

Discovering Customer Needs

To find out exactly what the customer wants (e.g., Do they want a wired earphone or a Bluetooth one?).

Increasing Sales & Profit

The ultimate goal is to sell more units and make money.

Building Brand Awareness

To make sure that when someone thinks of a product, they think of your company (e.g., thinking of "Maggi" instead of "noodles").

Creating Value & Retention

To provide a product that improves life and to keep old customers coming back to buy again.

D. Importance of Marketing

  • Connects Producer to Consumer:

    Without marketing, the best electronics product in the world will sit in a factory collecting dust. Marketing acts as the bridge.

  • Helps in Decision Making:

    It tells the entrepreneur what price to set, what features to add, and where to sell.

  • Generates Employment:

    Marketing creates jobs in advertising, sales, logistics, and digital media.

  • Improves Standard of Living:

    By marketing new, efficient products, it educates society about better ways of living.

2. Market Analysis and Market Research

PYQ Focus (Previous Year Question)

Discuss importance of market analysis. Meaning of 'market research'. Explain steps involved in marketing research process.

A. Importance of Market Analysis

Market Analysis is a deep study of the market's size, trends, growth rate, and profitability.

Why is it important? It acts as a reality check. Before an entrepreneur spends money on a startup, market analysis tells them if there is a real demand. It reduces the risk of failure, helps in understanding competitor strategies, and attracts investors.

B. What is Market Research?

Market Research is the systematic process of gathering, recording, and analyzing data about customers, competitors, and the market.

Market Research is like doing "detective work" before starting your business to find out if people will actually buy your product.
Electronics Ex: Finding out if customers prefer a smart-bulb controlled via Wi-Fi or Bluetooth, and its ideal price point.

C. Steps Involved in the Marketing Research Process

Memorize these steps in order for your exams

1

Define the Problem and Objectives

What exactly do you want to find out? (e.g., "Why are our electronics repair shop sales going down?")

2

Develop the Research Plan

How will you collect the data? Will you use surveys, online polls, or face-to-face interviews?

3

Collect the Data

This is the field work. You distribute surveys to customers or talk to them. (Gathering Primary and Secondary data).

4

Analyze the Data

You look at the collected information, put it into charts, and find patterns. (e.g., 80% of people said they want a home delivery repair service).

5

Present the Findings

Make a report of what you discovered.

6

Make the Decision

Based on the report, the entrepreneur takes action. (e.g., Starting a home-pickup service for broken electronics).

3. Identifying the Target Market

PYQ Focus (Previous Year Question)

What Questions help to identify the target market?

A. What is a Target Market?

Your product cannot be for "everybody." A Target Market is the specific group of customers at whom a company aims its products and marketing efforts.

Example: The target market for a high-end oscilloscope is electronics engineers and labs, not general households.

B. Questions to Help Identify the Target Market Crucial PYQ

To identify the exact target market, an entrepreneur must ask the following questions:

Who are they? (Demographics)

What is their age, gender, education, and occupation?

Where are they? (Geographics)

Do they live in cities, villages, or specific states? (e.g., AC stabilizers are targeted at areas with high voltage fluctuations).

What is their income level?

Can they afford a premium product, or do they need a budget-friendly option?

What are their pain points?

What problem are they trying to solve that your product can fix?

What are their buying habits?

Do they buy online (Amazon/Flipkart) or from physical electronics stores?

Who do they buy from?

Which competitor are they using right now, and why might they switch to you?

4. Competition Evaluation & Strategy

A. Competition Evaluation

Before launching a startup, you must know who you are fighting against.

  • Direct Competitors Businesses selling the exact same product. (e.g., Samsung vs. Apple).
  • Indirect Competitors Businesses selling different products that solve the same problem. (e.g., A cinema hall vs. Netflix).
How to evaluate: Entrepreneurs use SWOT Analysis (Strengths, Weaknesses, Opportunities, Threats) on their competitors to find out where the competitor is weak.

B. Strategy Development

Once you know your competitors, how do you beat them? You develop a strategy:

Cost Leadership Strategy

Make your product the cheapest in the market by reducing manufacturing costs. (e.g., Xiaomi phones in their early days).

Differentiation Strategy

Make your product unique or higher quality so people are willing to pay more. (e.g., Apple iPhone).

Niche/Focus Strategy

Focus on a very small, specific target market that big companies ignore. (e.g., Making specialized hearing aids for dogs).

5. Marketing and Accounting in Start-Ups

While marketing brings the money in, accounting keeps track of where the money goes.

A. Role of Accounting in Start-ups

Accounting is the process of recording, classifying, summarizing, and analyzing financial transactions of a business. It is called the "Language of Business."

Why it is needed:

1. Profit or Loss

It tells you if your startup is actually making money or burning cash.

2. Cash Flow

Startups often die because they run out of cash. Accounting tracks daily cash flow.

3. Legal Needs

The government requires proper accounting for filing taxes (GST, Income Tax).

Easy Word Explanation (The Car Analogy)

Marketing is the engine that drives the car forward.
Accounting is the dashboard (speedometer and fuel gauge) that tells you if you have enough petrol (money) to reach your destination.

6. Risk Analysis

PYQ Focus (Previous Year Question)

What is Risk Analysis in Business? Explain the purpose of risk analysis.

A. What is Risk Analysis?

Risk Analysis is the process of identifying, evaluating, and estimating the potential risks and uncertainties that could negatively affect a business, and then finding ways to minimize or manage them.

It is asking the question: "What is the worst that could happen?" and then making a backup plan for it.
Electronics Ex: Building a startup for Li-ion batteries. A massive risk is batteries catching fire due to a design flaw. Risk analysis identifies this danger early.

C. Types of Risks in Startups

  • Financial Risk

    Running out of money, customers not paying on time.

  • Market/Demand Risk

    What if people simply don't like the product?

  • Technology Risk

    What if a new technology makes your product outdated overnight? (e.g., CDs became useless when Pen Drives arrived).

B. Purpose of Risk Analysis

Why do entrepreneurs spend time analyzing risks?

To Prevent Financial Loss

By identifying risks early, a business saves millions. If a machine might break down, keep spare parts ready.

Contingency Plans

If Plan A fails due to a risk, Risk Analysis ensures you have a Plan B (backup plan) ready to go immediately.

Protect Brand Image

Releasing a faulty product destroys a brand. Risk analysis ensures strict quality checks are in place.

Ensure Survival

Startups often fail due to sudden shocks (policy changes, pandemics). Risk analysis prepares the business for survival.

To Secure Investment

Investors will only give you money if they see that you have identified all the risks and have a solid plan to tackle them.
